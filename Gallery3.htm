CHITAMBO DISTRICT IN RICE BUMPER HARVEST

 

The Government of Zambia through the Ministry of Agriculture has designated rice as one of the strategic food crops in the country apart from Cassava, Sorghum, Millet wheat and maize.RIce has also been identified to be one of the most profitable smallholder cash crops with potential to significally contribute to increased incomes and employment for many rural areas producers.

However, the production of rice does not meet the national demand which is estimated to be around 65,000MT. According to the 2018/19 crop forecast survey, rice production was estimated to decrease by 31.2 per cent to 29,584 metric tonnes from 43,063MT, thus creating an estimanted annual rice deficit of 35,000MT.

To address the problem, government through the Agricultural Productivity and Market Enhancement Project(APMEP) with fnancial support from the Global agricultural Food Support Program(GAFSP) has been promoting rice growing in Chitambo District through it's crop diversificaton sub-component.

The rice project that started with demonstration fields in 2015 in Chief Chitambo's area has now grown into a fully-fledged commercial venture as evidenced from the 567ha of land cultivated during the 2019/20 farming season.

 



The 567ha of rice fields comprises 358ha under the Luombwa Irrigation Scheme Company and 209ha belonging to seven rice clusters under the auspices of individual farmers who were supported with inputs such as seed and fertilizer.

2000MT of rice is known to translate into over K5million of monetary benefits to the district.

The Rice project has immennsely contributed to job creation and income generation in the distict which has a resulted into improved livelihoods adding that from the start of the project over 1000 people have been employed in the Scheme and over 90 individuals employed in the clusters.
