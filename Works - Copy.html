Chitambo District is one of the recently created districts in Central Province, is located some 357km North of Kabwe along Great North Road. It shares borders with Serenje, Samfya (Luapula Province), Lavushimanda (Muchinga Province) and the Democratic Republic of Congo. It has an estimated poulation of 48,861 people according too the Central Statistical Report of 2010.

 

INVESTMENT POTENTIAL

 

Agriculture and Agro-Processing

Chitambo District has a very good rainfall distribution throughout the rain season favouring any agriculture production. In addition, the distirct soil profile supports crop agriculture especially leguminous crops such as beans, soya beans and pigeon pea. Other crops that have high potential and can be grown in the district include; finger millet, sweet potatoes, groundnuts, sunflower, pumpkins, water melons, pineapples and vegetable crops that are grown off season. The District boasts of having over 15,000 small-scale farmers.

 

Tourism

Chitambo District is one of the uniquely endowed districts in terms of tourism in the province having the Kasanka National Park, which has the worlds largest mammal migration of several stra-colored fruit bats every october to december.

The District also hosts the David Livingstone Memorial Site where the remains of Dr. David Livingstone were buried.

The Bangweulu Westlands and plains are habitat of the Black Lechwe which is Endemic to the region and provides a hotspot for bird watching. In addition, The Luapula Bridge which is 3 Kilometer- long has a scenic view of the Luapula River. The District has the Nsalu caves which contain some of the best examples of Africa's schematic rock paintings. The Distrct is also home to Kings Harbor Pleasure Resort the newly built council lodge which offers various servicingThe District offers diverse opportuniies for investments ranging from lodges to campsites and tour operating services.

Mining

The District's mining sector remains unexplored. The geographical surveys conducted shows presence of minerals listed below.

  • Gold
  • Manganese
  • China Clay
  • Iron ore

There is high potential for Investment in quarrying especially with a steady rise in the constuction industry owing to the District being under significant infastructure developments since it's creation few years ago.

Manufacturing

With abundant land and all year round sunshine, the district offers opportunities in solar power generation. The district has good supply of electricity, good road network connecting the raw material producing areas and marketing centers. With ongoing investments in infastructure development such as roads and railway line, the district has a good link to the Democratic Republic Of Congo and an investor would have easy access to the Central, Eastern and Northern sections of Zambia.

 

DISTRICT COMPARATIVE ADVANTAGES

Chitambo District has the following comparative advantages:

  • Favourable rainfall distribution throughout the rain season, crop and livestock agriculture production.
  • It's the only place is the world with the largest bat colony , the worlds largest mammal migraton at The Kasanka National Park a massive potential to promote tourism
  • The District has vast tracks of alluvial soils that can support organic agriculture.
  • The District is traversed by a number of streams and rivers with water running throughout the year, dam formation for water storage can be done to support irrigtion farming.
  • The District is endowed with unique natural and historical sites, that can promote tourism.
