CHITAMBO DISTRICT OF CENTRAL PROVINCE is a recent district that was created in 2012 by His excellence Mr. Michael Chilufya Sata the late. It borders with Serenje, Samfya (Luapula Province), Lavushimanda (Muchinga province) and the Democratic Republic of Congo.

Since its creation as a district, Chitambo has a number of Developments, as the agenda of the government is to develop all corners of Zambia; Chitambo has not been left behind.

Chitambo is in the category of fast developing districts in the country with a rise in population estimated at 48,861 people.

Chitambo has potential in all sectors of the economy; economic activities have now increased since the creation of the District.

 

INVESTMENT POTENTIAL

 

AGRICULTURE AND AGRO-PROCESSING

Chitambo District has a very good rainfall distribution throughout the rain season favouring any agriculture production. In addition, the distirct soil profile supports crop agriculture especially leguminous crops such as beans, soya beans and pigeon pea. Other crops that have high potential and can be grown in the district include; finger millet, sweet potatoes, groundnuts, sunflower, pumpkins, water melons, pineapples and vegetable crops that are grown off season. The District boasts of having over 15,000 small-scale farmers.

With the infrastructure development Chitambo is currently enjoying, it will be the most attractive new district in Central province Zambia, recently National Pension Scheme Authority (NAPSA) Embarked on the construction of 50 houses which will be completed by the end of the year.

The Council Secretary Mr. Edgar C Mulwanda and the local authority at large are delighted with the project undertaken as it will steer up development in the province. Once this project is complete it will add beauty to the town.

Government department are will soon begin building their offices on the new site with local authority putting up office block and Resort for visitors in the district.

AGRICULTURE SECTOR

Chitambo District has a favorable climate which makes it very suitable to grow different kinds of crops. Chitambo District is commonly known for the produce of crops such as maize, Irish potatoes, sweet potatoes, rice, Cassava, Beans, Soya Beans, Ground nuts, Sunflower, Pumpkins, Water Melon and many other crops. The district boasts of more than 16000 small scale farmers.

In 2020, Chitambo Town Council started growing rice, with 25ha and in the 2020 -2021 season the local authority increased the capacity to 50ha with an estimate of 3000 by 40kgs bags to be harvested.

The Council Secretary Mr. Edgar C Mulwanda stated that if the agriculture sector could be exploited well, Chitambo could develop drastically. Chitambo Rice farming was not exploited much by the local people in recent past but ever since the creation of the district and the commitment of the local authority to commercialize the crop, the number of locals taking keen interest in the commodity is growing, with the availability of Land and favorable climate Chitambo district will be the hub of Rice production in the country.

With the high number of small scale farmers and wide variety of crops produced in the district, opportunities exists for investment in the agro processing, and packaging of the crops produced as evidenced in the conduction of the cassava milling plant which is set to be commissioned in June.

FISHING ACTIVITIES

Chitambo district is rich in River banks with plenty of water all year round, with the good rainfall distribution that the district receives the rivers are overwhelmed with good water beyond the rainy season.

There has been some worries from the ministry of fisheries and livestock about the fishing methods used by the local people in which mosquitoe nets are used which is posing a threat to degrading fish in these river banks. The ministry is doing everything possible to educate and imposing fish bans during the fish breeding season.
