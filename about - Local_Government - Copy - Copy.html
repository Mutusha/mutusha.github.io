WATER SUPPLY AND SANITATION

 

WASTE MANAGEMENT UNIT

The Government of the Republic of Zambia(GRZ) through the Ministry of Local Government and Housing launched the National Rural Water Supply and Sanitation Programme. In November 2007. This was developed to achieve the Milennium Development goals(MDGs) for Water supply and sanitation and the targets set in Zambia's Vision 2030 of 100 per cent access to safe water supply and sanitaton for all users for wealth creation and improved livelihood by 2030.
Main functions

  1. Management of the disposal site (currently in Chung)al" style="color:#212529;mso-margin-top-alt:auto;mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto;text-align:justify;line-height:200%;mso-list:l0 level1 lfo1; tab-stops:list 36.0pt">Waste registration and charging of fees for waste disposal.
  2. Collection of waste from the Central Business District
  3. Collection of fees from Central Business District (Waste Management District ‘’D’’).
  4. Secondary Collection of waste from unplanned settlements (Peri-urban areas) and fees from community based organisations involved from Primary Collection in these areas.
  5. Contracting of private waste collection companies to collect waste in Waste Management Districts (WMDs) on a franchise basis.
  6. Contracts Management and supervision of private waste contractors’(franchise holders)
  7. Inspection of all waste management activities and enforcement of waste management regulations(esp. the Waste Management by-laws)
  8. Develop and oversee the implementation of long –term solid waste management plans on behalf of Lusaka City Council (LCC) which will include resource mobilization for capital projects and institutional support.
  9. Conduct periodic Environmental Audits of the Existing SWM Strategies and provide direction on future implementation strategies.
  10. Advise Management on policy matters relating to solid waste management in City of Lusaka.
  11. Maintains contact with all waste generator but especially with those waste generators that have a direct relation with the Waste Management Unit(WMU) through a contract or memorandum of understanding

Waste Management Advisory center
The Waste Management Unit (WMU) Advisory center through the office of the Customer Relation Office offers advice to residents on ways of avoiding and reducing waste, effectively reducing on the collection bills. The Unit also offers technical advice on recycling and material recovery for both resident and up-coming entrepreneurs. For any queries contact the Waste Management Unit(WMU) through wmu@microlink.zm
