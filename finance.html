KEY FUNCTIONS OF THE FINANCE DEPARTMENT

i) To maximize income generating activities and improve revenue colection efficiency.

ii) To enhance transperancy and financial accountability through having updated and audited financial statements, management account and budgetory control that allows expenditure minimization.

iii) To improve on the zmanagemnet of Council Estates and ensure tenancy agreements are made with all Tenants in commercial and residential properties.

iv) To propose strategies for improved revenue collection.

v) To prepare updated and audited financial statements.

vi) Make periodic statements in terms of expenditure

vii) Provide financial advice to the members the council, the officers and the general public through the councilors.

viii) Ensure that controls which guide the council (monitors its financial performance by ensuring that accounting rules and procedures) are in place.

ix) Renders regular and fair account of stewardship and performance of the council money.

 

FUNCTIONS OF THE FINANCE COMMITTEE

  • Monitors activities and policies that regulate, control and manage finances of the council
  • Monitors revenue collection.
  • Monitors expenditure of the council.
  • Advises on and monitors application of rates and levies.
  • Checks irregularities regarding financial transactions.
  • Support development and implementation of budgets.

 

THE FUNCTIONS OF THE REVENUE COLLECTORS

  • Collect revenues of various forms from tax payers and other subordinate revenue collectors such as market masters bus station agents using a general receipt book.
  • Account for revenue collected by making use of appropriate cash book used or recording all the revenue collected.
  • Make periodical remittance of all revenue collected to control office cashier.
  • Receive all necessary receipt and market fee books from central office for in his/her area of duties.
  • Account for the use of the general receipt book and market fees book.
  • Produce periodic revenue reports.
  • Work hand in hand with other stakeholder especially the traditional leaders in all matters affecting revenue collection.

 

SOURCES OF REVENUE

Local authorities can rise revenues either internally or externally. Internal revenue sources are those which a local authority can raise in the form of local taxes.

External sources of finance include government grants, loans and bilateral and multilateral aid.

Councils also have powers to impose levies, fees and charges on the citizenry by way of by-laws or council resolution.
